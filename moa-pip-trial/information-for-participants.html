He Oranga mō te whanau | MOA-PIP Trial

Improving outcomes for people with hip and knee OA and co-morbidities

Who are the treatments are for:

If you are between the ages of 35-70 years

AND

have hip or knee pain, aching or soreness most days, that is made worse by doing more activity or work

AND

have at least one other chronic health condition such as:

  • type 2 diabetes or prediabetes,
  • heart disease (heart failure or coronary heart disease),
  • hypertension (high blood pressure)
  • symptoms of mild-moderate depression
  • Having other chronic health conditions does not exclude your participation.

What you will be asked to do?

  1. Research Nurse will talk to you either face to face or over the phone to:
    1. Enrol and obtain your consent including some personal details like your age and ethnicity, questionnaire about your health
    2. Assessment includes tests of leg strength and walking speed, measure of blood pressure, height and weight, one blood test
    3. The assessment will be used to determine whether the new treatment program is better than what is currently offered for hip and knee pain.
    4. 3 monthly assessments for up to a year after finishing the new treatment programme.
  2. Practice nurse and health coach
    1. Six visits total over six months
    2. Primary care nurse visit will take 30 minutes for the first visit clinical assessment with practice nurse for BP, Height, weight, maybe HbA1c blood test, nutrition education and goal setting
    3. Health coach visits will take about 15 minutes to talk about food and healthy choices and set three achievable lifestyle goals.
  3. Physiotherapist from Cape Physio
    1. 12 sessions total over 6 month
    2. Expected exercise therapy will take an hour each session
    3. You will be advised to carry on doing the exercise therapy on your own between physiotherapy sessions.
